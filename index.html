--- layout: default --- The number of COVID-19 cases \(R (t)\) is expressed as Richards' growth function $$R(t) = \dfrac{L}{\left(1+e^{-k \nu (t-t_0)}\right)^\frac{1}{\nu}}$$ were presended, where \(G_R(t)\) expressed as follow $$G_R(t)=\log{\dfrac{1}{K(t)^{1+\nu}}\dfrac{d K(t)}{d t}}=\log{\dfrac{k}{L^\nu}}-k\nu (t-t_0). $$ The following is the result of the world and each countries infected cases R (t) dynamics model and its comparison with observed experimentally results for the determination of parameters using the least squares method [4]: $$ \min{\sum_{i=1}^{N}\left(R(t_i,a)-y_i\right)^2}$$ where R (t, a) is the Richards growth function $$R(t) = \dfrac{L}{\left(1+e^{-k \nu (t-t_0)}\right)^\frac{1}{\nu}}$$ or bi-Richards growth function $$R(t) = \dfrac{L_1}{\left(1+e^{-k_1 \nu_1 (t-t_{01})}\right)^\frac{1}{\nu_1}}+\dfrac{L_2}{\left(1+e^{-k_2 \nu_2 (t-t_{02})}\right)^\frac{1}{\nu_2}}$$ Finally, active model is expressed as follow $$A(t_N)=\sum\limits_{i=N-k}^{N}\left.\dfrac{d R(t)}{dt}\right\vert_{t_{i}}$$ where \(t_{N}\) is the current day of modeled active data, \(k\) is the amount of days to the past.
